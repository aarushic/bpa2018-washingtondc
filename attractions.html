Big Bus Tours

610 10th St NW, Washington, DC 20001
  • The Double Decker and Open-Top Buses allow members to visit many of the landmarks with unlimited access for 1 or 2 days. On Big Bus Tours you can hop off to visit various landmarks at your own pace, including the White House, Capitol Hill, the Jefferson Memorial, and the Lincoln Memorial. The Big Bus Tour takes you to the city's most popular attractions, including over 40 stops at key locations.
  • There are three routes to choose from including the red loop, blue loop, and yellow loop. The Red Loop is considered the official tour of the National Mall and stops at museums, monuments, and memorials. The tour includes live guides and pre-recorded commentary that is available in 8 languages. The Blue Loop tour takes you across the Potomac River to the Arlington National Cemetery and Pentagon City and also includes pre-recorded commentary. The Yellow Loop tour takes you to Georgetown but includes pre-recorded commentary in English only.
  • Classic Ticket Child: $35 Adult: $49 Premium Ticket Child: $45 Adult: $59 Deluxe Ticket Child: $49.50 Adult: $62.10

National Air and Space Museum

600 Independence Ave SW, Washington, DC 20560
  • The Museum on the National Mall has many famous icons of flight including the original Wright Flyer, the Spirit of St. Louis, the Mercury Friendship 7 capsule that was piloted by John Glenn, SpaceShipOne, and a touchable lunar rock. There are free guided tours are offered every day at 10:30 am and 1:00 pm. Many interactive activities are available for visitors of younger age in the "How Things Fly" gallery and Design Hangar.
  • Visitors can also watch an IMAX movie or Planetarium show. There is also a flight simulator which has a small cost. Audio tours are available for those that want to gain insight while exploring the Museum's collection by themselves. Tours are offered in languages such as English, French, Mandarin, and Spanish There are also fees for this tour but discounts are offered for groups of two or more.
  • Hours: 10:00 am - 5:30 pm. It is open Daily Except December 25. The admission Free, No Tickets Required

Lincoln Memorial

2 Lincoln Memorial Cir NW, Washington, DC 20037
  • The Lincoln Memorial commemorates the 16th President, Abraham Lincoln. The Memorial includes marble columns surrounded by greenery, which was inspired by ancient Greek temples. There are 36 columns, each representing a state in the U.S. at the date of the death of Lincoln. The memorial is 190 feet long, 119 feet wide, and almost 100 feet high. Etched into the wall of the memorial is the quote, "In this temple, as in the hearts of the people for whom he saved the Union, the memory of Abraham Lincoln is enshrined forever." Underneath the quote is a 19-foot tall, 175-ton statue of President Lincoln.
  • The most convenient way to reach the Lincoln Memorial is either by Metrorail or Metrobus. The nearest Metro stations are Foggy Bottom and Smithsonian, which are both on the Orange, Blue and Silver lines. If you are traveling by Metrobus, it is best to take the 32 route, 34 route, or 36 route. There are no fees for this attraction.
  • The Memorial is open 24 hours a day and National Park Service Rangers are on duty from 9:30 am to 11:30 pm every and are available to ask questions. No fee is required.

Washington Monument

2 2 15th St. NW Washington, DC 200024
  • The Washington Monument is meant to honor George Washington, the commander-in-chief of the Continental Army and the first President of the United States. It is maintained by the National Park Service and is located on the center of the National Mall between the U.S. Capitol and the Lincoln Memorial.
  • The most convenient way to get to the Washington Monument is to take the Metro. The two closest Metro stops are Federal Triangle and Smithsonian, which are both on the Blue, Orange, and Silver lines. If you traveling by Metrobus, it is best to take DC Circulator National Mall route or the Metrobus routes 32, 34 or 36. If you are driving, visitor parking is available on Ohio Drive, between the Lincoln and Jefferson memorials.
  • The Memorial is open 24 hours a day and National Park Service Rangers are on duty from 9:30 am to 11:30 pm every and are available to ask questions. No fee is required.

Martin Luther King, Jr. Memorial

1964 Independence Ave SW, Washington, DC 20024
  • The Martin Luther King, Jr. Memorial is located next to the Franklin Delano Roosevelt Memorial and across from the Lincoln and Jefferson Memorials. The Memorial commemorates Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. who was one of the most prominent leaders of the Civil Rights Movement in the 1960s.
  • The easiest way to reach the Martin Luther King, Jr. Memorial is by either Metrobus or the Metrorail. If traveling by the Metrobus, use the 32 route, 34 route, or 36 route. The closest Metrorail stop is Smithsonian, which is on the Orange, Blue, and Silver lines.
  • The Memorial is open 24 hours a day and National Park Service Rangers are on duty from 9:30 am to 11:30 pm every and are available to ask questions. No fee is required.

Franklin Delano Roosevelt Memorial

1850 West Basin Dr SW, Washington, DC 20242
  • The memorial honors the 32nd president of the United States, Franklin Delano Roosevelt, and is made up of four outdoor rooms which each represent one of the four terms of President Roosevelt as Commander in Chief.
  • The most convenient way to get to the memorial is by taking the Metrorail, the two closest Metro stops are Federal Triangle and Smithsonian which are both on the Blue, Orange and Silver lines. If you are planning on traveling by bus, it is best to take the DC Circulator National Mall route. If you are driving, there is visitor parking available on Ohio Drive, which is between the Lincoln and Jefferson memorials.
  • The Memorial is open 24 hours a day and National Park Service Rangers are on duty from 9:30 am to 11:30 pm every and are available to ask questions. No fee is required.

White House

1600 Pennsylvania Ave NW, Washington, DC 20500
  • Public tour requests must be submitted through one Member of Congress. You can submit a request up to 3 months ahead but no less than 21 days. These self-guided tours are available from 7:30 a.m. to 11:30 a.m. Tuesday through Thursday, 7:30 a.m. to 1:30 p.m. Fridays and Saturdays (excluding federal holidays or unless otherwise noted). Tour hours will be extended when possible based on the official White House schedule. No fee is required.
  • Guests 18 years of age or older will be required to present a valid, government-issued photo identification. All foreign nationals must present their passport. All other forms of foreign identification will not be accepted. All information submitted must exactly match the government-issued photo ID you will present when arriving at the White House. The following forms of photo ID are acceptable: A valid government-issued United States identification card (e.g. drivers license, military ID, etc.) or a valid United States or other official government-issued passports.
  • Prohibited items include, but are not limited to, the following: Video Recorders, Handbags, book bags, backpacks or purses, Food or beverages, tobacco products, personal grooming items, Strollers, Any pointed objects, Aerosol containers, Guns, ammunition, fireworks, electric stun guns, mace, martial arts weapons/devices, or knives of any size. Video cameras including any action camcorders, cameras with detachable lenses, tablets, tripods, monopods and camera sticks are not permitted. Flash photography or live stream as well as talking or texting on cellular phones are not permitted.

FBI Building

935 Pennsylvania Ave NW Washington, DC 20535
  • Visiting the FBI Building provides the opportunity for a self-guided tour at the FBI Headquarters in Washington, D.C. On the tour you will get to learn how the Bureau operates and will get a look inside the FBI organization investigative divisions, laboratory resources, professional occupations, and more. The tour features multimedia exhibits, content, and artifacts.
  • Visits to The FBI Building must be scheduled in advance. Similar to arranging a visit to the White House, to visit the FBI Building you have to go through your Congressperson. Tours can be scheduled on business days 9am - 3pm, which must be done at least 4 weeks in advance.
  • All applicants for a tour must provide the following: name, date of birth, and social security number. FBI tours are only available to US citizens and green card holders. There is no storage at the FBI Headquarters. The following items are not permitted into the building: backpacks, diaper bags, fanny packs, briefcases, luggage, computer and camera bags, or bags and purses larger than a clutch, point-and-shoot cameras, SLR cameras, or video recorders; cell phone cameras are permitted but must be on airplane mode during the visit, strollers, food, beverages, tobacco products, personal grooming items, pointed objects, aerosol containers, guns, ammunition, fireworks, electric stun guns, mace, martial arts weapons/devices, or knives.

Pentagon

Washington, DC 22202
  • The Pentagon is the headquarters for the United States Department of Defense. It is open for official tours through the Pentagon Tours program. Pentagon tours must be reserved at least 14 days in advance and no more than 90 days in advance. Tours are conducted Monday-Thursday (excluding federal holidays) from 10 a.m.- 4 p.m. and from 12 - 4 p.m. on Fridays. You can make a tour request online.
  • Tours are 60 minutes and cover about 1.5 miles inside the Pentagon, which is one of the largest office buildings in the world. Tours include the history of the four branches of the military and the opportunity to see the indoor memorial near the Sept. 11 crash site and the Sept. 11 Memorial chapel, as well as the Hall of Heroes (featuring the names of all the Medal of Honor recipients) and a number of other military displays.
  • There is no public parking at The Pentagon. The Pentagon can be reached via its own dedicated Metro stop on the Blue and Yellow lines or you may choose to park at Pentagon City Mall. Once you arrived, check in at the Pentagon Tours window near the Metro entrance. You must check-in 60 minutes prior to the scheduled tour to allow time for security. You will be asked to present your tour confirmation email and photo ID. All visitors will go through security scanners. Large bags, including backpacks, shopping bags and camera bags are NOT allowed. Cell phone, cameras, recording devices, other electronic devices and photography are not permitted inside the building.
